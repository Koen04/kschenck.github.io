About Me

I am an undergraduate student studying astronomy and astrophysics at the Florida Institute of Technology. My research focuses on the evolution of planetary systems, particularly hot Saturn exoplanets, galactic astrophysics, and cosmology. I am most passionate about neutrino Astrophysics and the origin of our universe.

Research Interests

Magnetic Amplification of Photoevaporation in Hot Saturns

This project explores the magnetic coupling and ohmic heating effects driving atmospheric loss and photoevaporation in hot Saturn exoplanets. Using MESA-based simulations, we model coupled dynamo activity and ionization-driven mass loss in hot Saturns to better constrain their observed radial inflation.

Mapping Nebular Gas Structure Through Stellar Population Filtering in the Orion Nebula

I study the radial/spatial distribution and color–magnitude evolution of molecular gas and stellar objects in the Orion Nebula Cluster using color magnitude diagrams and optical and Hα imaging to probe age gradients and spatial clustering across the Trapezium region.

Neutrino Astrophysics: An expansion of Pantheon+

My current work involves modeling neutrino-driven expansion in the early universe and examining constraints on relic neutrino backgrounds from cosmological data sets such as Pantheon+SHOES. This project is ongoing.
