About Me

I am an undergraduate student studying astronomy and astrophysics at the Florida Institute of Technology. My research focuses on the evolution of planetary systems, particularly hot Saturn exoplanets, pre-main-sequence stellar populations in GMC, and cosmology. I am most passionate about neutrino Astrophysics and the origins of our universe.

Research Interests

Magnetic Amplification of Photoevaporation in Hot Saturns

This project explores the magnetic coupling and ohmic heating effects driving atmospheric loss in sub-Jovian exoplanets. Using MESA-based simulations, I model coupled dynamo activity and ionization-driven mass loss in hot Saturns to better constrain their observed radius inflation.

Pre-Main-Sequence Stellar Populations in M42

I study the radial distribution and color–magnitude evolution of young stellar objects in the Orion Nebula Cluster using optical and Hα imaging to probe age gradients and spatial clustering across the Trapezium region.

Neutrino Astrophysics and Early-Universe Cosmology

My current work involves modeling neutrino-driven expansion in the early universe and examining constraints on relic neutrino backgrounds from cosmological data sets such as Pantheon+SHOES. This project is ongoing.
