About Me

I am an undergraduate student studying astronomy and astrophysics at the Florida Institute of Technology. My research focuses on the evolution of planetary systems, particularly hot Saturn exoplanets, pre-main-sequence stellar populations in GMC, and cosmology. I am most passionate about neutrino Astrophysics and the origins of our universe.

Research Interests

My research focuses on the evolution of planetary systems, particularly hot Saturn exoplanets, and pre-main-sequence stellar populations in giant molecular clouds. I also work on cosmology, exploring early-universe models and neutrino astrophysics.

Recent Work: “Magnetic Amplification of Photoevaporation: Coupled Dynamo and Ohmic Heating in Hot Saturns” — Icarus, 2025 [PDF]
